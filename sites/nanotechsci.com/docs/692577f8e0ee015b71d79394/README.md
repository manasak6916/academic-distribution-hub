# SCP-Nano: Revolutionizing Nanocarrier Tracking with Single-Cell Resolution and AI

## Summary
This article describes Single-Cell Profiling of Nanocarriers (SCP-Nano), an integrated pipeline that revolutionizes nanocarrier tracking. By combining optimized tissue clearing, high-resolution light-sheet microscopy, and a custom deep learning model, SCP-Nano maps the biodistribution of therapeutics throughout an entire organism at single-cell resolution. The technology boasts exceptional sensitivity, capable of detecting nanocarriers at doses 100 to 1,000 times lower than conventional imaging methods like PET or CT. This allows for analysis at clinically relevant concentrations, such as those used in mRNA vaccines. The platform is generalizable across various nanocarriers, including lipid nanoparticles (LNPs), adeno-associated viruses (AAVs), and DNA origami. Key applications include identifying specific cellular targets and detecting unintended off-target accumulation, such as LNPs in heart tissue. SCP-Nano provides a powerful tool for accelerating the development of safer, more precise nanomedicines by offering an unprecedented view of their in vivo behavior.

## Extended Summary
This article provides a comprehensive overview of Single-Cell Profiling of Nanocarriers (SCP-Nano), a groundbreaking technology designed to overcome the limitations of conventional biodistribution analysis. Developed by researchers in Munich, SCP-Nano integrates three core components: an optimized DISCO-based tissue clearing protocol that preserves fluorescence, high-speed light-sheet microscopy for whole-body 3D imaging, and a custom-trained 3D U-Net deep learning model for automated single-cell quantification. This pipeline achieves unparalleled sensitivity, detecting nanocarriers at doses as low as 0.0005 mg/kg, which is 100-1,000 times lower than methods like PET or CT and aligns with clinically relevant vaccine doses. The platform's single-cell resolution enables researchers to move beyond organ-level analysis to identify the specific cell types that interact with nanocarriers. The article details the experimental workflow, from nanocarrier administration in mice to AI-powered data analysis, which can process terabytes of image data to identify millions of cell-targeting events. Applications have yielded critical insights, including the discovery that intramuscularly injected mRNA-LNPs can accumulate in heart tissue, with associated proteomic changes suggesting potential side effects. Furthermore, SCP-Nano has identified novel tropisms, such as an AAV variant targeting adipose tissue and DNA origami structures targeting immune cells. By providing a robust, generalizable platform for precise biodistribution mapping, SCP-Nano is positioned to de-risk and accelerate the development of safer, more effective nanocarrier-based therapeutics.
