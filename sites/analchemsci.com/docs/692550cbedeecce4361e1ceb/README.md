# 2025 Analytical Chemistry Research Trends: A Comprehensive Overview of Innovations, Applications, and Sustainable Practices

## Summary
This comprehensive overview details the pivotal research trends shaping analytical chemistry in 2025, emphasizing a paradigm shift from traditional green chemistry toward strong sustainability and circularity. Key advancements include the adoption of Green Sample Preparation techniques and sustainable solvents like ionic liquids and deep eutectic solvents. The article introduces the White Analytical Chemistry framework, which balances environmental impact with analytical performance and practicality. Technological frontiers are expanding through the integration of CRISPR-Cas systems and aptamers in clinical diagnostics, the use of Boron Nitride Nanosheets and Molecularly Imprinted Polymers for advanced sensing, and the application of multidimensional chromatography for complex mixture analysis. Furthermore, the development of 3D and 4D printed lab-on-a-chip devices and wearable point-of-care sensors is revolutionizing decentralized diagnostics. The guide also highlights the importance of automated sample preparation, strategies to mitigate the environmental rebound effect, and rigorous method validation using tools like the AGREE metric to ensure data integrity and sustainable laboratory practices.

## Extended Summary
The 2025 landscape of analytical chemistry is undergoing a profound transformation driven by the dual imperatives of technological innovation and ecological sustainability. This comprehensive whitepaper outlines the transition from weak sustainability models to strong sustainability and circularity, challenging laboratories to adopt disruptive, resource-efficient practices. At the forefront of this shift is Green Sample Preparation, which leverages miniaturization, alternative energy sources like ultrasound, and green solvents such as ionic liquids and deep eutectic solvents to minimize environmental footprints without compromising analytical efficacy. The article details the White Analytical Chemistry RGB framework, a holistic model evaluating methods across environmental, analytical, and practical dimensions.

Technological frontiers are rapidly expanding across multiple domains. In clinical diagnostics, the convergence of CRISPR-Cas systems, aptamers, and nanoscale measurement platforms is enabling ultra-sensitive, decentralized point-of-care and wearable sensors. Materials science innovations highlight Boron Nitride Nanosheets and Molecularly Imprinted Polymers as superior platforms for solid-phase extraction and sensing. For complex mixture analysis, multidimensional hyphenated techniques, specifically 2D-LC and GCxGC, offer unprecedented resolving power, while single-cell ICP-MS is pushing the boundaries of ultra-trace elemental analysis in biomedical and semiconductor applications. Additionally, 3D and 4D printing technologies are revolutionizing the fabrication of customized solid-phase extraction cartridges and lab-on-a-chip devices.

To support these advancements, the article emphasizes the necessity of automated, high-throughput sample preparation to reduce human error and solvent waste. Crucially, it addresses the rebound effect, wherein efficiency gains inadvertently lead to increased resource consumption, proposing policy and technological mitigations. Finally, the guide underscores the critical role of rigorous method validation, aligning with ICH and Eurachem guidelines, to ensure data integrity. It advocates for the use of quantitative greenness assessment tools, particularly the AGREE metric, to objectively evaluate and improve the sustainability of modern analytical procedures.
